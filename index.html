About

Biography

Ahmed Nabwy is a researcher and writer focusing on state theory, political geography, and the structural power dynamics of the Middle East. He grounds his analysis in political economy and advanced state theory, treating physical space, urban infrastructures, and borders as active sites of capital accumulation, dispossession, and class struggle. He holds a Master of Arts from Hitit University and a Bachelor of Arts from Cairo University, and is currently advancing his doctoral research.

His central research project, "Power, Space, and Representation: Everyday Politics and Urban Resistance—A Case Study of Cairo," examines the material conditions and cultural forces driving political action within urban centers. Alongside his primary research, he is developing a broader theoretical framework on the formation and mechanics of the modern state in the Arab world, placing contemporary political phenomena into their long historical and structural contexts.

As an analytical writer and public-facing scholar, Nabwy bridges academic rigor and critical journalism. He regularly publishes long-form analytical essays on regional power structures—with a specific focus on Iran, Turkey, and the Israeli-Palestinian conflict—for prominent platforms including Al Jazeera's Midan and Jadaliyya. He also directs the independent digital publication "Margins", bringing critical theory and structural analysis to a wider intellectual audience.
